Father of Glory,

we come to you in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ,

from whom every family on heaven and on earth takes its name

We thank you for the grace you have given us in Christ

that you have chosen us, called us, forgiven us

and sealed us with your Holy Spirit

We praise you for the hope to which you have called us

and for the riches of your glorious inheritance promised us

We ask that you would give us the Spirit of wisdom and revelation

so that we may know you better

Enlighten the eyes of our hearts

that we may know the hope of your calling

and the surpassing greatness of your power towards us who believe

the same power that raised Christ from the dead

Strengthen us with power through your Spirit in our inner being

so that Christ may dwell in our hearts through faith

Root and ground us in love

that we may grasp together with all the saints

the breadth, length, height and depth of the love of Christ

and know this love that surpasses knowledge

so that we may be filled with all your fullness

Make us worthy of the calling we have received

Lead us to walk in a manner worthy of you Lord

bearing fruit in every good work

growing in the knowledge of you as our God

and being strengthened with all power for endurance patience and joy

Guard us in your truth,

Sanctify us by your Word, for your Word is truth

Keep us from the evil one

And unite us in heart and mind

So the world may see that we belong to you

Fill us with love that abounds more and more,

with knowledge and discernment

so that we may approve of what is excellent

and pure and blameless on the day of Christ

filled with the fruit of righteousness that comes through your son Jesus

to the glory and praise of God the Father

Make us a people who pray without ceasing

who rejoice in hope,

and steadfast in suffering,

and faithful in love

May our lives proclaim the gospel

our unity reflect your glory

and our obedience bring honour to your name

Now to you who are able to do immeasurably more

than all we ask or imagine

according to your power at work within us

to you be glory in the church and in Christ Jesus

throughout all generations, forever and ever

= the revival

there’s a scene describing the ordinary people of the revival:

The scene was almost indescribable. Tier upon tier of men and women filled every inch of space. Those who could not gain admittance stood outside and listened at the doors. Others rushed to the windows, where almost every word was audible. When, at seven o’clock, the service began, quite 2,000 people must have been present. The enthusiasm was unbounded. Women sang and shouted till the perspiration ran down their faces, and men jumped up one after the other to testify. One told in quivering accents the story of a drunken life. A working [man] spoke like a practiced orator: and one can imagine what a note the testimony of a converted gypsy woman struck when, dressed in her best, she told of her reformation and repentance. At ten o’clock the meeting had lost none of its ardor. Prayer after prayer went up. Time and again the four ministers who stood in the pulpit attempted to start a hymn, but it was all in vain. The revival had taken hold of the people, and even Evan Roberts cannot hold it in check. The latest convert is a policeman, who, after complaining that people had gone mad after religion, so that there was nothing to do, went to see for himself, and bursting into tears, confessed the error of his ways, and repented

they said of the revival there were no leaders: no preaching, no order, no hymnbooks, no choirs, no organs, no collections, and, finally, no advertising;

The last person to control the meeting in any way is Mr Evan Roberts
